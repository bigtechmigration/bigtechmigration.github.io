The Problem

European companies are funnelling sensitive customer data into US-based cloud providers — exposing themselves to foreign surveillance laws, unpredictable pricing, and regulatory risk.

⚠

Data Sovereignty Risk

US Cloud Act and FISA 702 allow US authorities to access data stored by American companies, regardless of where the servers are located.

↑

Escalating Costs

Big tech cloud providers use aggressive pricing strategies — low entry costs that escalate dramatically as you scale, with complex billing that obscures true spend.

Regulatory Pressure

GDPR enforcement is intensifying. Recent rulings have questioned the legality of EU-US data transfers, putting companies relying on US providers at risk.
